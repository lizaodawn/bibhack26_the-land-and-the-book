Project Overview

To evoke the multi-sensory experience of travel, we aim to present travelogues textually, visually and spatially. For our project, we chose to do a deep dive into “The Land and the Book”, a nineteenth century travelogue of Palestine and a Biblical narrative by the American Protestant missionary William McClure Thomson (1806-1894). The work combines travel description, ethnographic observation, and biblical commentary based on Thomson’s travels in Ottoman-era Syria and Palestine. To explore this travelogue computationally, we created an interactive map interface with clickable locations points, displaying image and text pairings from the travelogue.
